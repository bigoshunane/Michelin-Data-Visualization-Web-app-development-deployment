Michelin stars around the world.

The Michelin Star Guide "aims to make driving, tourism and the search for unforgettable experiences available to all." Since 1926, the team at Michelin has been awarding exceptional restaurants with coveted star ratings. Mystery diners assess the "quality of the ingredients used, mastery of flavor and cooking techniques, the personality of the chef in his cuisine, value for money and consistency between visits." Learn more about the distinctive ratings and more below.

About the Michelin star awards

Bib Gourmand

Not quite a star, but most definitely not a consolation prize. This rating recognizes friendly establishments that serve good food at moderate prices.

One star

High quality cooking. Worth a stop!

Two Stars

Excellent cooking, worth a detour!

Three stars

Exceptional cuisine, worth a special journey!
